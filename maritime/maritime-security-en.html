Why Maritime Security
is Essential Now

In 2026, the security environment surrounding the maritime industry has changed dramatically.
With strengthened regulations and intensified cyber attacks, business continuity is difficult without proper measures.

⚖️
2026 Enforcement

IMO Regulations & NIS2 Directive

The new regulations by the International Maritime Organization (IMO) and the EU's NIS2 directive make cybersecurity measures a legal obligation for maritime companies. Fines for violations can reach up to €10 million.

🎯
400% Increase

Attacks on Maritime Industry

Ransomware attacks on the maritime industry have quadrupled compared to 2025. Major companies like Maersk and COSCO have been affected. Average damage exceeds ¥500 million.

⛴️
Vessel Operations Halt

Business Continuity Crisis

Cyber attacks can stop vessel operation systems, causing billions of yen in losses from weeks of business shutdown. Insurance coverage increasingly denied.

2026 Major Maritime Security Threats

🏴‍☠️ Vessel OT System Attacks

Intrusions into navigation systems like GPS, AIS, and ECDIS. Spoofed location data and disabled collision avoidance systems directly threaten vessel safety.

🔗 Supply Chain Attacks

Intrusions into port systems, logistics management systems, and customs systems. The entire supply chain halts, causing severe impact on global logistics.

💰 Ransomware Attacks

Encryption of vessel management systems, booking systems, and customer databases. Business cannot resume without paying ransom. Insurance often doesn't cover.

📡 Satellite Communication Vulnerabilities

Intrusions into vessel satellite communications like VSAT. Remote control, communication interception, data theft. Vessels on open seas are especially vulnerable.
