Regulatory Compliance

Compliance is Mandatory

Since January 2021, all international voyaging vessels under the ISM Code must integrate cyber risks into their SMS (Safety Management System).
Non-compliance may result in PSC (Port State Control) detention or operational suspension.

Maritime cyber security regulations are rapidly evolving. Review this timeline to understand your compliance requirements.

January 2021

IMO MSC.428(98) In Effect

Cyber risk management mandatory for all vessels. Integration of cyber risks into SMS required.

July 2024

IACS E26/E27 In Effect

Cyber security design requirements for new builds. Secure design and verification of vessel systems mandatory.

October 2024

EU NIS2 Directive In Effect

Maritime companies designated as critical infrastructure. NIS2 compliance mandatory for operators in EU waters.

July 2025

US Coast Guard Regulation

Cyber Security Officer appointment mandatory. Applies to vessels calling at US ports.

January 2026

US Crew Training Requirement

Cyber security training mandatory for crew. Regular training implementation and record-keeping required.

July 2027

US Plan Approval Requirement

Pre-approval of cyber security plans required. Coast Guard review and approval process initiated.
